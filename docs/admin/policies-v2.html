    Course Policies

    • The instructor reserves the right to change the syllabus at any time; you will be notified of any changes.
    • Take responsibility for your own learning. Active learners tend to understand more deeply and perform better.
    • Teams of three collaborate on weekly worksheets

    We will annouce a worksheet on Wednesday 17:00, which include the video, the hands-on lab, and textbook readings. Please work on these content on your own. Write and share your learning notes on a HackMD document with your teammates. The hands-on lab contains several questions for you to think about (marked by Q1, Q2 …). You will run some commands and provide screenshots of the output (marked by S1, S2 …).

    We encourage you to explore beyond the taught content by asking extra and questions writing your own discoveries. We call this shio, short for Shio Dan Ji Lie. You may optionally put your SDJL in the report.

    The structure of the report is:

    1. video/textbook: learning notes, required but not graded
    2. video/textbook: Shio, optional
    3. hands-on lab: screenshot, required
    4. hands-on lab: answers to questions, required
    5. hands-on lab: what i learn, required
    6. hands-on lab: Shio, optional

    teammate can discuss, share screenshot and write together within team, each teammate must write their own “what i learn” please follow the course’s official HackMD Report Template) The hackmd document should be submitted by next wednesday 17:00

    Submission format: Switch HackMD to View mode and use the browser’s Print → Save as PDF. Submit the PDF (one per team).

    Each week’s report counts for 1 semester point, the grading rule is specified in the hackmd template.

    shio card

    On week N+2, we will select some very nice shio from your week N report (both video shio and hands-on lab shio), and openly highlight them on Wednesday’s lecture time (shio time). If your team’s shio is highlighted, your team will receive a “shio card.”

    • 只要你的 shio 被選到，你們這組將獲得一張燒蛋卡。 (As long as your shio gets selected, your team will receive a “shio card.”)

    • 一張燒蛋卡等同學期總成績1分，每組最多可加10分學期總成績 (One Shio Card equals one point in semester grade. A team gets maximum 10 points)

    • 學期末獲得前10名燒蛋卡的小組，可加學期總成績6分。

    • 學期末獲得前10~20名燒蛋卡的小組，可加學期總成績3分。 (At the end of the semester, the team with the most shio cards will get extra 5, 3, 1 points.)

    Weekly cycle

    • Week N - (Wed): Worksheet released at 17:00
    • Week N+1 - (Mon): Team Time (optional attendance)
    • Week N+1 - (Wed): In-class quiz (cover: Week N) (required attendance)
    • Week N+1 - (Wed): Report for Week N due at 17:00
    • Week N+2 — (Wed): shio time for Week N

    Monday: Team Time

    • 10:10 AM: Team 1–15 (Teams 16–30 after midterm)
    • 11:10 AM: Team 16–30 (Teams 1–15 after midterm)

    Monday’s session is your team time. Attendance is optional. You can use this time to work on the lab worksheet that was released the previous Wednesday. The instructor and TAs will be available to answer questions and provide guidance.

    Wednesday: Full Class + Quiz

    Everyone gathers for a full-class in-person session. The instructor will either give a lecture or highlight selected teams’ insightful shio (the shio time). In the middle of the class, you will take a 30-minute quiz based on the previous week’s lectures and labs. We will discuss the quiz answers afterward.

    Throughout the semester, we will have 9 quizzes. The 7 highest scores will count toward your final grade.

    Attendance

    If you are unable to attend a quiz, midterm or final exam due to force majeure (不可抗力因素), you may submit a leave request through the school’s leave application system. If the leave request is approved, you may apply for a make-up exam.

    TA checklist (pass/fail): - [ ] Sections appear in the required order (Video Notes → Video SDJL → Lab Q/A → Lab SDJL).
    - [ ] All (screenshot?) items are present and legible.
    - [ ] Video SDJL contains at least one substantive question + exploration.
    - [ ] Lab Q/A answers are complete.

    Integrity note: Do not share final answers or screenshots across teams. The report should reflect your team’s work. Quizzes ensure each person truly understands the material.

    There are two xv6 programming labs that require live demos:

    • At demo time, the TA randomly picks teammate to answer questions about your code (switch file you change, how a function work, etc.)
    • If the selected teammate cannot answer, the teammate can save, but receive half of point.

    Academic Integrity

    Collaborate thoughtfully; protect your opportunity to learn.

    • Discuss concepts only freely within your team and on Discord. Offer hints and reasoning; don’t tell others final answers you write in the report.
      • Allowed: conceptual discussion, short logs, URLs to document,
      • Not allowed: final answers, full code, or graded (screenshot?) images.
    • Do not share code or final answers across teams (any medium).
    • Your team’s submissions must be your own work; every member is responsible for understanding what is submitted.
    • Quizzes, midterm, and final are strictly individual.

    Use of LLMs

    ChatGPT or other kinds of LLM can be a great tool, but it will limit your learning if you rely on it too much.
    You spend your time here not just to get grade, but to learn and make this knowledge your own. Here’s my advice:

    • Learn how to ask: LLMs are most helpful when you know what to ask it and how to interpret its result, NOT when you copy+paste questions and then copy+paste the answer it provides.

    • Get definition: If you’re asking questions about OS definitions or explanations about terminology, that’s great! LLM can be a great Google replacement.

    • For programming lab assignments, try to write your code independently first. If you get stuck, it’s okay to ask an LLM for guidance on function usage or debugging help, but make sure you understand the solution. Never submit code that you don’t thoroughly understand.

    • If you’re brainstorming approaches to a problem, always think on your own first and then ask an LLM to expand your ideas. You don’t want to limit your thinking by relying on AI first. Always test what you understand.

    • Do not auto-generate graded content for your team report (e.g., do not paste AI-written “discoveries” or answers). Grammar fix is ok, but you must write the main content.

    • LLMs are not permitted during quizzes, midterm, or final.
